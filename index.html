About Sofiia

Sofiia began her career at 18, working in Barcelona as a junior data engineer. Her role focused on increasing the KPIs of client organizations by building automation systems that made processes more time- and cost-efficient. This experience improved her hard and soft skills: while working daily with SQL & Python and expanding her knowledge in BI development, she also learned the importance of well-coordinated teamwork and professional client communication and support.

Sofiia is passionate about solving real-world challenges through technology. A clear example is her internship in Washington, D.C., where she partnered with the World Bank to develop a platform that predicts high-risk areas vulnerable to natural disasters. Her main contributions included training AI models and developing interactive web maps using satellite data from NASA's open sources. She later applied these skills in the NASA Space Apps Challenge and built the Urban Growth Platform in partnership with the International Space Alliance.

Next

Currently, Sofiia is exploring the intersection of technology and human behavior, focusing on behavior-shaping, emotionality, and decision-making. She is pursuing a double degree in Computer and Social Sciences, studying over four years in four different regions of the world: North America, Asia, South America, and Europe (currently in San Francisco, California). Driven by a desire to deeply understand local cultures, she is not only a native Ukrainian speaker but also fluent in English and Spanish, and is currently learning Japanese. This globally immersive approach enriches her perspective and supports a culturally adaptive professional style.
